Coir Fibre(Brown)

Coir fibre is the versatile fibrous material found between the hard, internal shell and the outer coat of a coconut. Depending upon the freshness and maturity of coconut's husk, extracted fibre's color, quality and purpose will get differed. They are white or brown in color. The coir fiber is elastic enough to twist without breaking and it holds a curl as though permanently waved.We are one of the leading Coconut Coir Fibre Suppliers based in India.

It is extracted out of matured brown coconut. Mature coir fibers contain more lignin and less cellulose than fibers such as flax and cotton and so are stronger but less flexible. The coir fiber is elastic enough to twist without breaking and it holds a curl as though permanently waved.

It is used for various applications like making Curled Coir Rope used in manufacturing coir mattresses, floor mats, door mats, brushes, rubberized coir pads, coir cushions, carpet under lays, seat cushions,for erosion control in river banks and hillsides and for insulating drainage pipes

Brown coir pads are sprayed with rubber latex, which bonds the fibres together (rubberized coir) and is extensively used as upholstery padding for the automobile industry.

Mushroom Farming

Cocopeat is an excellent medium for mushroom cultivtion. For increased yields, it can be used alone or as a blend combined with frozen black peat from a reliable source.

First cocopeat was soaked in water for 24hours before mixing. Initially individual casting material such as farm yard manure,sawdust,cocopeat,sand, vermi compost were sterilized in a horizantal autoclave at a temperature of 100 degree celsious for 20 minutes. Sterilized casing material were taken out to cool down to normal room temperature by spreading on well cleaned cement floor. After that all the casing material are mixed by the required proportions followed by casting application

Types of mushrooms

you can grow hydroponically are: nameko, shiitake, oyster, lionâ€™s mane, button, maitake, cinnamon cap and enokitake.

Process of mushroom cultivtion involves

Spawning,Casing,Harvesting

the simple method of filling the compost in bags and mixing the spawn 2-3 inches deep in the substrate. I add 1 kg spawn in every25 kg of substrate and it spreads quickly.After spawning tie the mouth of the bags and leave it for atleast 2-3 weeks.Spray water on floor and walls before putting the bags.Now close the room.

After 3 weeks you will see that the mycellium has spread thoroughly.The color of compost will change to pinkish brown and white threads will be seen. At this stage comes the casing.

Button mushrooms do not come to the fruiting stage unless some casing is applied.There are many options for the casing.Take cocopeat bricks, leave them in a tub of water for a few hours.After that they are easily converted to cocopeat and fluffed out. Prepare the casing as soon as the spawned bags are put in the room for few weeks.After fluffing it pasteurize it .

Take 500 ml of formalin in 10 litre of water and sprinkle it on cocopeat and cover with a plastic sheet for 2 days.after that open it so that the smell of formalin is gone.It should be used after atleast 15 days..when the mycellium spread is complete in the bags open the mouth and put 2-3" of this pasteurised cocopeat on the surface and again close the room for 2 days so that the mycellium comes on the surface. You will see very small pins on the surface.

Now is the time to ventilate the room.Maintain humidity by spraying water on walls and floors. Dont splash on the bags rather mist the surface only.These small pins grow into button mushrooms of 2-3"diameter. Pick them when the gills on the underside are still covered for best taste.The flushes of mushrooms repeat after every 7-10 days until the nutrients are exhausted,normally 3-5 good flushes can be obtained.

A turf soil testing laboratory

Mix several combinations of sand and cocopeat until they have a rootzone within the specified range of Moisture Retention,Porosity and Infiltration Rate.

The laboratory then specifies that rootzone mix, based either on:

  • organic matter content - the rootzone is specified according to the OM content of the optimum mix (eg: 1.5% OM)
  • volumetric rate - the rootzone is specified according to the volume of Cocopeat to be mixed with a volume of loose sand (eg: 10% amendment rate)
  • gravimetric rate - the rootzone is specified according to a weight of dry Cocopeat to be mixed to a certain weight of sand(eg: 1.2% by weight)
  • Moisture Retention specification - the rootzone is specified according to the final Moisture Retention required (eg: 14%, plus or minus 1%).

Get the Cocopeat delivered to the sand quarry in bulk form. Blend the Cocopeat and sand in the required percentages by volume. Minimum recommended rate of 15% Cocopeat by volume.

Mixing of Organic Material into Sand

Organic material should not be mixed by rotary hoeing it into the top of the green as this will lead to a very uneven distribution of the material throughout the profile. In some areas the mixture could be 200mm deep and in others it could be 100mm resulting in differing nutrient and water contents, and uneven root growth.

Mix your organic Cocopeat, into the sand at the quarry source by the supplier. This way the organic mix will be more uniform and you will be able to spread the blend more accurately and be assured of a high quality profile throughout the green.

Rootzone Construction

Cocopeat amended is sent to the Green construction site. Amended sand is spread and evenout to create seeded rootzone profile.
